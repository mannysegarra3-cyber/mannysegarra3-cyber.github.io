Foundation

The Three Founding Pillars

Every prompt package, every question bank, every coaching tip is built on these three convictions. They are not principles recited in a workshop. They are the operating system.

PILLAR 01

The Human Always Comes First

The Sprint will end. The product will ship or it won't. None of that is the work. The work is the person sitting across from you right now — carrying something you cannot see yet.

"A teammate came to me struggling — not with code, with his cousin's addiction. He came to me because he knew I cared about him beyond just the code he wrote. Eleven years later, when I got laid off, he gave me a like. That is the return on human investment."

PILLAR 02

People Over Profit

The company pays you in money. That is the transaction. What your teammates give you back — sometimes years later, in a message you did not expect — is gold that goes straight to your heart.

"Know the difference. Choose accordingly."

PILLAR 03

Grow the Person in Front of You

Ask yourself after every interaction — did I make that person larger or smaller today? That question is your compass. Everything else is navigation.

"Growing a person is an act of love — professional, with boundaries, disciplined love. It is the highest use of your time in the room."

Rigor

Question Validation Methodology

Every question in every bank is validated against four frameworks and tested against three design tests. Table stakes to you is learning for the teams — the questions earn their place.

📊

Bloom's Revised Taxonomy

Questions are mapped across cognitive levels from remember to create — ensuring depth, not just coverage. No question banks flat on the knowledge tier.

🧠

Vygotsky's ZPD

Zone of Proximal Development scaffolding — each question sits at the edge of what the SM can do alone, inviting growth without overwhelm.

🔄

Nonaka & Takeuchi SECI

Quiz Bank A externalizes individual knowledge. Field Bank B socializes it to the team. The dual-bank architecture traces back to the 2008 Epiphany KMS thesis.

🎯

Deming's PDSA

Plan-Do-Study-Act is embedded in the four-phase structure of every question bank. Continuous improvement is not a principle here — it is the architecture.
